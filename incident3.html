Headline

Aurangabad: The much awaited work on the six-lane Beed bypass road and Jalna road is likely to begin in a couple of months. While conducting a review meeting of officials of all concerned departments, Aurangabad MP Chandrakant Khaire on Saturday announced that the work on both the sites will begin soon after the end of monsoon. The meeting was attended by Om Prakash Bakoria, municipal commissioner, Nidhi Pandey, district collector, U G Chamargore, project director of National Highway Authority of India (NHAI) and other senior officers. The NHAI has begun ground work on the two mega projects which are expected to change the face of the city. The NHAI is building 14.25 km long Jalna road with an investment of Rs 400 crore, which is part of the national highway number 211 from Nagar Naka to Cambridge school. Similarly, they are also constructing a 14km stretch of Beed bypass road between Zalta Fata and Mahanubhav Ashram with an expenditure of Rs 250 crore. The NHAI has proposed to build four flyovers on Jalna road and three on Beed bypass. In addition to this, an underpass has been proposed on two locations in Jalna roadnear Dohoot hospital and Amarpreet hotel chowk. Tenders for both the roads is likely to be floated in a month.
